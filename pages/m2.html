Contextual Inquiry

(Milestone 2)

Contextual Inquiry Process

Team members worked together to craft a semi-structured interview guide containing questions about social media and music consumption experiences and habits. Using this guide, each member of the group interviewed three participants for a total of twelve interviews. Participants generally belonged to the social circles of the interviewers, as this made sense in a COVID-affected world. Once an interview was scheduled, a follow up Zoom invitation or reminder was sent to the participant on the day of the appointment. Interviews were audio-recorded with the permission of the interviewee. Participant demographics are shown below. The data gleaned from this process was then parsed and categorized into an informative affinity map, which will guide the design process as we seek to keep user needs and desires at the forefront of our minds.

Interview Guide

The following questions were used for our contextual inquiry interviews:

Demographic Information
- What is your name?
- Do you identify as male or female?
- How old are you?
- What is your occupation?
- What devices do you mainly use to access music?
- What devices do you mainly use to get on social media?
Social Media Usage
- What current social media apps do you use?
- What do you like about them? What do you dislike about them?
- How much time do you spend on social media each day?
- Do you like when your friends share what they are listening to on social media?
- When you think of social media, how do you feel about the interactions that happen there?
Music Consumption & Sharing
- What current music streaming platforms do you use?
- Do you use the free or paid version (if applicable)? Why is that?
- What do you like about them?
- What do you dislike about them?
- How many hours a week do you spend on music apps?
- How often do you listen to music with other people?
- Do you share music with your friends?
- What does the process of sharing music with them look like?
- How do your friends share music with you, if they do?
- Do you feel that it’s difficult to find people who share your music tastes?
- Does your current music app connect you with others of similar music tastes?
- Do you feel that the music app(s) you currently use facilitate social interaction well?
- Would you enjoy a music streaming platform that connects you with people of similar music tastes?
- How do you find and choose new music to listen to? (If they need help, prompt with “Friends suggestions? Application suggestions? Radio?”)
- Would you be interested in connecting with new people with similar music tastes as you? Or just with friends you already know?

We also gave our participants a few scenarios to gauge their interest in some potential features.

Scenario 1
You are at a coffee shop listening to the music on your phone on an application that fosters music sharing. There is a cute person across the room, but they have headphones on. You can see on the app that they are listening to a song from a genre you like. After a little while, you get a notification that someone has a song suggestion for you. You open it up to see the cute person’s profile picture next to the title of a song. You can accept or decline it. In this case, you accept. After listening to the song, a chat window appears for you to optionally start a conversation or give feedback about their suggestion. Assuming you have the ability to turn this feature on and off, would you or any of your friends enjoy sharing music in this way? Why or why not?
Scenario 2
You and your friends or loved ones are separated during an internship over the summer. You all enjoy listening to and sharing music, but given the distance between you, it seems difficult. You open a music app on your phone and start a “studio” room that your friends can join from the applications on their phones if they are online. Once the room is joined, you can all work together to curate a playlist, listen to music, and chat simultaneously. Do you think that you or anyone you know would enjoy using a feature like that? Why or why not?
Scenario 3
You are unable to physically be with a friend/loved one, but you both enjoy listening to the same type of music. You open a music app on your phone and see that the other person is offline. You send them song suggestions you think they would like for them to listen to when they return. Do you think that you or anyone you know would enjoy using a feature like that? Why or why not?
