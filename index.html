Project Focus

(Milestone 1)

Scope

Our goal is to explore a playful musical application that supports both positive and meaningful social interactions between young adults. Over the course of the semester, we will research potential features that augment the human experience and create a prototype showcasing them. Possible features may involve group music curation, soundtracks based on biometric data, and networking based on shared tastes. The process will involve user research, creating personas, and designing both low and high fidelity prototypes to be tested by the target audience.

Stakeholders

The stakeholders we identified are as follows:

Listeners

Goals
Find new music, find friends with similar tastes, adjust or maintain mood, listen uninterupted.
Concerns
Learning curve, cost.
Communication
Via design, robust help features, and a clear onboarding process.

Artists

Goals
Connect with fans, share music, improve profit.
Concerns
Violations of copyright, ease of use.
Communication
Via their own user accounts or through agents.

Developers

Goals
Create a high-value, functional application on time.
Concerns
Development complexity, time requirements.
Communication
Via daily scrum meetings. (Agile methodology)

Designers

Goals
Create an outstanding, beautiful, intuitive experience.
Concerns
Design trade-offs for the sake of cost or time; Meeting user needs.
Communication
Via daily scrum meetings. (Agile methodology)

Business Partners

Goals
Profitable, popular product with staying power.
Concerns
Investment always runs the risk of being unprofitable.
Communication
Via interval meetings containing financial information and product progress in an understandable, engaging format.

General Approach

The first step to our approach was to identify a problem statement. We brainstormed various ideas for problems we could solve in our domain of social media. One of the things we noted in our discussion is that many applications in this domain have become mires of negativity and unhealthy comparison. We also believe that music is a positive experience for most people, as it provides a therapeutic retreat from the stresses of life or enables one to ruminate on emotions. From these realizations, we decided to move in the direction of designing a social media application that facilitates positive interaction and sharing based on music.

At present, we are in the process of solidifying who all of our stakeholders will be for this project. So far, we have discussed and included those listed in the above section. The next step in our plan is to conduct a contextual inquiry. We will perform research on potential features based on the desires and habits of musical listeners, especially as it relates to sharing music with others. We plan to glean this information mainly through surveys, interviews, and other forms of user-research that are COVID-friendly. From this data, we will develop an affinity diagram, and create personas that capture our user base.

Next, we will design prototypes of our application, starting with low-fidelity iterations. A high-fidelity, interactive prototype will be the final product. This will be tested by representatives of our user base to determine whether the experience is pleasant, useful, and fulfilling. As a team, we will be dividing work based on our strengths. Throughout the process, we will be constantly evaluating and revising our work to ensure that valuable, quality deliverables are presented to our stakeholders.

Team Covenant

Team Four hereby establishes the following covenant:

Meetings
- Members will attend meetings on time and be active contributors to the topic at hand.
- Members will inform colleagues if it is necessary to miss a meeting 24 hours in advance, excepting medical or familial emergencies.
- If a meeting is missed, the absent member should follow-up with the team as soon as possible.
Communication
- Members are expected to communicate regularly, as their schedules allow.
Project Work
- Members are expected to contribute to each step of the project process, though it is also expected that there may be areas of strengths and weaknesses. This is okay.
- Members should seek to improve their weak areas and emphasize their strengths. Work should be divided as equitably as possible.
- If a member is struggling to fulfill a project need, they should reach out early on instead of waiting until the last minute to seek help.
Decisions
- Members will seek to make decisions for the project based on consensus. In cases where a consensus may not be reached, a Â¾ majority will be needed to move forward.
Interpersonal Tensions
- Member dialogue shall remain respectful in order to maintain an environment that encourages sharing.
- Disagreements or conflicts will be discussed with all members present to determine the best way to resolve the matter.
